Beer School

  1. Lager
    • Adjuct Lagers

      • Such as your Budwieser, Miller, or Yuenglueng

    • German Lagers

      • These are darker like Dunkels or Doppelbocks

  2. Ale
    • Stouts

      • A dark style of beer that can have a number of ingredients added to it. Most commonly are notes of coffee, chocolate, or vanilla

    • IPA

      • This style of beer can normally taste very bitter and earthy; for the seasoned beer drinkers

    • Saison

      • A floral style of beer more likely have notes of herbs or spices and can be similar in taste with IPA's

    • Pale Ale

      • A much more subtle approach to a hoppy beer than the IPA and can go down easy

    • Sour

      • Not for everyone but can be a nice change of pace; usually contain fruity notes and tart to the pallete

    • Barleywine

      • Almost always high ABV and can range from a large variety of flavors; Every Barleywine tastes different

    • Wheat Beer

      • An "easy drinkin'" style of beer, usually low ABV and contains notes of citrus or stone fruits

  3. Cider

    4. Ciders are made in a similar fashion to beer but are 100% fruity apple goodness. No hops included.
